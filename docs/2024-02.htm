Statistical Consulting Unit wins SHINE! Quality of Service Award

Congratulations to the Statistical Consulting Unit (SCU) for winning the 2023 SHINE! ‘Quality of Service’ team award! The award salutes truly outstanding service by a team that has shown professional behaviour, consistency of service and skill in providing analysis and insights that helped researchers achieve their goals. 
In 2023, the team’s statisticians trained over 1000 researchers and undertook 518 consultations. To enhance research quality and impact, the team have developed an interconnected ecosystem of support involving consultations, online resources, and workshops. Twelve modular workshops focus on versatile, software-agnostic, practical and user-friendly workflows, allowing researchers to customize their training.
Contact us for all your statistical consulting, training and analysis queries - we look forward to assisting you!

Foundational statistical workshops - planning your research

7 February Hybrid workshops: in-person or Zoom 

To support you with planning your research and prepare your grant applications we are offering a full day of our 'foundation' workshops. This day of workshops will be hosted by the Brain and Mind Centre, and is open to attendees from all faculties.
Our software-agnostic, 90-minute workshops feature practical workflows and run on Wednesday 7th February at Camperdown campus and via Zoom. Attend “Research Essentials – Analysing your Data”, “Experimental Design” and "Power and sample size" to plan your study and set yourself up for research success!

Supercomputing at Sydney - Artemis

6 February: in-person workshops 

Artemis is the University of Sydney’s High Performance Computer (HPC). Come and learn how to use it and how it operates with Sydney’s “Research Data Store" in our "Introduction to Artemis HPC" and "Data transfer and Research Data Storage (RDS) for HPC" workshops on 6 February.
If your computational work is the bottle-neck for getting your research done (slow laptop, no storage, etc) then these are the courses for you. 
Come along to learn some simple strategies for accelerating your workloads that can be applied to common tools like matlab, python, R, and more!
