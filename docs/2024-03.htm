News and Announcements

First SIH Statistical workshops for 2024 were hosted at the Brain and Mind Centre 

Have you had a chance to review the programme for this year's SIH workshops? We're excited to announce that 45 workshops will be conducted by the statistical consulting team, in addition to the many other training offerings provided by SIH.
This year, we decided to kick things off in a unique way. Instead of holding our workshops at the usual location in the Merewether building, we took a journey across the Camperdown/Darlington campus to the Brain and Mind Centre at Mallett St. Here, we were graciously hosted by the Early-Mid Career Research committee as we presented our foundational series of workshops.
The day was a resounding success, with positive feedback from our hosts and other attendees. The SIH team was thrilled to commence another year of training alongside our valued colleagues. The foundational series equips participants with practical advice and valuable tools for navigating the complicated landscape of quantitative research. It begins with our Research Essentials workshop, covering topics ranging from data management to results presentation. Next, our Experimental (and study) Design workshop delves into considerations for crafting a robust and impactful research study. Lastly, the Power and Sample Size Calculation workshop provides comprehensive guidance on choosing a sample size to adequately power your study. 
If you missed out on attending the foundational statistical workshops, fear not! We'll be presenting them four more times this year, with the next session scheduled for March 26th. All other SIH statistical workshops for the first half of the year are already listed in the training calendar, allowing you to select those that best suit your needs from our modular training framework. These workshops are concise, lasting only 90 minutes, and are presented in person (with Zoom available for those unable to attend in person). Best of all, they are completely free for all Sydney University higher degree research students and staff. Don't miss out—sign up today!

R-Ladies Sydney is back!

Are you itching to dive into the world of R but don't know where to start? Are you longing to connect with fellow R aficionados and share your favourite R packages? Well, look no further. After a brief hiatus the Sydney chapter of R-Ladies Global has been revived!
Our mission? To encourage, inspire, and empower people of under-represented genders in the R community, helping them unleash their programming potential. Whether you are a student, researcher or a corporate professional, R-Ladies Sydney welcomes you with open arms
Join us on the first Wednesday of every month for our engaging meetup sessions, both online and in-person at the sleek Nous Group offices in the heart of the city. Thanks to our generous sponsors at Nous Group, light nibbles are on us!
So far, our sessions have been nothing short of exciting! From networking meetups where we exchanged ideas, to code & tell events where we troubleshooted tricky R code dilemmas and shared invaluable R packages. In our latest session, "RYouWithMe?", we kicked off a four-part series tailored for beginners and coding enthusiasts alike. We took it back to basics, exploring everything from installing R and R packages to customising our R settings. And the adventure doesn't end there! Our upcoming sessions will delve into the art of data cleaning, data visualisation and creating R markdown files to collaborate seamlessly with your peers.
Connect with us on social media to stay updated about these exciting events and more. We hope to see you there! #RLadiesSydney #RYouReady

Hacky Hour

20 March, 3-4pm: Zoom

Hacky Hour is a regular meetup where researchers – students, staff, and university affiliates – can gather in a social environment to collaborate and receive research support. SIH Masterclass - Mastering Data Visualisation Design 

18 April, 3-4pm: Zoom 

Data visualization is more than just making plots. It is a powerful way to showcase astounding results, communicate information, tell stories, and influence decisions. But how can we design impactful visuals that are clear, compelling, and captivating? 
In this Masterclass, we will showcase the principles of and best practices for data visualization design. We will explore how to choose the right visualisation type with a goal in mind and how to tailor images for a specific audience. We'll talk about how colour, shape, size, layout and interactivity can make our plots, infographics and charts stand out. 
Whether you're writing your Honours thesis, crafting your first paper or a seasoned researcher with an H-Index greater than 60, this workshop is for you (And just to make clear: this one has no coding involved!)!
