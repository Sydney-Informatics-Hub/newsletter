SIH welcomes their new Deputy Director

SIH is very pleased to welcome Associate Professor Zhiyong Wang as Deputy Director. 
Zhiyong Wang is an Associate Professor and Director of the Multimedia Computing Laboratory at the School of Computer Science, The University of Sydney. As a leading expert in the field of multimedia computing, he has focused his research on computing innovation for better searching, understanding, and creating multimedia data.
His research has been applied across different domains such as agriculture, environment, health, and medicine, and is also the foundation to many emerging fields such as digital twin and metaverse.
He will bring extensive experience and expertise on data science, artificial intelligence and research computing to SIH. Here's what he had to say about his time with SIH so far:
“Like many colleagues in the University, I have witnessed and tremendously benefited from the great services SIH has provided to accelerate the University’s research excellence. Since joining SIH, I have enjoyed the discussions with many SIH members and stakeholders in various meetings, and am grateful for the support from SIH colleagues. I am excited about opportunities to work together to make SIH a hub of computing, data, and intelligence to better strengthen and empower the research excellence of the University.”

Advance your RNAseq knowledge: comprehensive resources for differential gene expression analysis 

RNA sequencing (RNAseq) is a widely adopted and powerful technique used to gain insights into gene activity. On October 11-12th, SIH's Dr. Nandan Deshpande and Dr Georgina Samaha hosted an introductory RNAseq workshop with the Australian BioCommons. Attendees explored Pawsey's Nimbus cloud platform, converted raw sequence reads into analysis-ready count data using the nf-core/rnaseq workflow and identified differentially expressed genes and functional enrichments in RStudio.  
Missed it? No problem! We made all workshop materials available for you. You can use the containerised RStudio environment, an R notebook, and self-directed materials to practice RNAseq analysis at your own pace.
Part of the Australian BioCommons Bring Your Own Data Platforms Project, this workshop was an extension of our introductory RNAseq webinar Getting Started with RNAseq: Transforming Raw Reads into Biological Insights.
