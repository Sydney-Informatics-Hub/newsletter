Survey Design & Analysis Day

May 24 Hybrid workshops: in-person or Zoom 

Thanks to university-supported online platforms like Redcap and Qualtrics, delivering surveys to research participants is easier than ever. Asking the right questions within your surveys can be more of a challenge. Is it easy for your participants to understand what you are asking? Will analysis of the responses help you answer your research questions? Is your survey well designed? 
On May 24, Sydney Informatics Hub presents a day of workshops relevant to researchers who want to upskill in survey techniques. Each workshop is 90 minutes long and provides practical advice on methods used frequently in survey design and analysis. Surveys I covers design and analysis considerations common to most surveys. Surveys II delves into more advanced topics including assessing reliability and creating new indices using multiple questions.
Register now for one or both surveys workshops to ensure that your next survey will yield valuable information for your research.

SIH Masterclass: Distributional Regression modeling: Raise your research to the next level 

25 May: in-person or Zoom

Elevate your research and come to the SIH masterclass on distributional regression! The classical linear model and its generalized extension (GLM) are widely used in applied statistics. However, required model assumptions are frequently not met, resulting in estimates of effects that can be biased.
This masterclass will outline how distributional regression, an increasingly popular tool in regression analysis, overcomes these limitations by providing the ability to link distributional parameters beyond the mean to additive predictors, for a practically unlimited choice of response distributions. Register now!

How: in-person and Zoom

Exploring Digital Text Collections with Juxtorpus: A Taster Webinar on the Latest ATAP Text Analysis Tool 

May 25: in-person or Zoom

Join us for a hybrid webinar on the latest addition to the suite of ATAP text analysis tools - Juxtorpus. Developed to provide a unified framework for managing and exploring text contents and metadata, Juxtorpus offers a Corpus package that enables flexible building, exploration, and slicing of your corpus while maintaining its shape. It also offers a Jux package that allows for easy comparison and highlighting of differences between any two corpora with tools and visualisations that come off-the-shelf. During the session, we'll also show you how to use other ATAP tools in combination with the Corpus to create a reusable workflow that will boost your analysis capabilities.  
We invite anyone interested in learning how to handle and analyse their digital text collections to join us. No programming knowledge or skills are required, and there will be minimal hands-on opportunities during this session. Register to reserve your spot today. 
When: 25 May, 10:30am – 12pm

Pro-tips for scaling data processing and analysis on HPCs 

May 31, online

Processing large computational datasets requires specialised compute resources like those provided by High Performance Computing (HPC) infrastructures such as the University’s Artemis HPC. These infrastructures offer the computational scale and efficiency needed to process and analyse your data quickly and efficiently. If you’re new to HPC or an infrequent user, learning all the jargon and understanding the system’s expectations and processes, can be overwhelming.  
Join us for a webinar on the 31st of May when we’ll provide pro-tips for scaling workflows to HPC with our partners at the Australian BioCommons, National Computational Infrastructure in Canberra and Pawsey Supercomputing Research Centre in Perth. This webinar follows on from previous talks addressing what national computational facilities are available to Australian researchers and how to put together a compelling NCMAS grant application. We’ll explain how to make the most of HPC infrastructures and deal with common pain points, with a particular focus on bioinformatics.
