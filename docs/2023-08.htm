RezBaz Sydney 2023 – Call for Expressions of Interest   

ResBaz (from ‘Research Bazaar’) is a part conference/part festival community building event where researchers across various disciplines can learn, share knowledge, network and experience cutting edge technologies, methods and techniques. This year ResBaz Sydney is being hosted at the University of Sydney from 26-28 September as the first in-person Sydney event since 2019.
ResBaz Sydney 2023 is open to all NSW/ACT researchers. One of the core values of ResBaz is People over Technology, which in part recognises that a good way to ensure knowledge retention is to build community. 
This year’s program will contain workshops, talks and demonstrations across each of the four themes:  
  • Python 
  • R Programming 
  • HPC / AI 
  • Community building & wellness
The organising committee is keen to receive expressions of interest from researchers and support staff who are willing to help run workshops or give a talk or a demo showcasing their research – particularly if it includes use of digital tools/skills.
To register your interest, please complete the EOI form

NCI Adapter Scheme for HPC hours 

Move your workflows from Artemis to the Gadi Supercomputer at NCI

The merit-based Adapter Allocation Scheme allows for more flexible access to high-performance computing, cloud and storage resources. Adapter is designed to identify meritorious research projects which need flexible compute access over a short period.
This scheme caters for those users who need smaller compute and data resources in short bursts, rather than those who need millions of compute hours over the course of a whole year. Early-career researchers, PhD students and applicants new to merit allocation schemes can benefit from the Adapter Scheme!  Applications for Q4 2023 are now open!

SIH Masterclass: Sharing your research: setting up a personal blog or portfolio website     

24 August: Zoom

A personal blog or website gives you a permanent home for your research profile – it can be a great way to share your portfolio, publicize your research, and share your knowledge with the world. In this workshop we’ll give an overview of how to set up a website using free tools like Pelican or blogdown, which give you complete control of your content. We’ll discuss all the steps from creating your site, to hosting it, to making sure your content is shareable and searchable.
NOTE: this workshop will focus on creating your own website using free and open source tools (e.g. Pelican, blogdown, Jekyll), hosting it for free via GitHub. These tools generally require some basic familiarity with Python, R and/or terminal commands. Tools like Squarespace or Wordpress that handle everything for you are also a great option – but not the focus of this workshop. Register now!
When: 24 August, 3-4pm
Where: Zoom only

Demystifying statistical modelling 

30 August Hybrid workshop: in-person or Zoom 

Do you get confused about the differences between linear and logistic regression or ANOVA, ANCOVA, repeated measures and mixed models?
On August 30 we will deliver three workshops from our modular statistical training system to demystify these terms and show how all of these align in the one Linear Models framework. Linear Models Module 1 explains how the common analyses of regression, ANOVA, ANCOVA and repeated measures can all be done using the same Linear Models workflow. Module 2 then extends this to the Generalised Linear Model (GLM) framework for non-normal distributions such as Logistic and Count/Poisson regression. Aligning all these different methods into the same box with the same generic workflow makes it much easier to understand and remember how to apply them, while the practical examples provided give clear instructions for later use. The final module on the day, Statistical Model Building, explores the issues around more complex statistical modelling to build multivariable models with many predictors.

BioCommons workshop: RNASeq: reads to differential genes and pathways  

September & October: Introductory RNA sequencing training 

Are you a biologist or clinician interested in applying gene expression analysis to your research? RNA sequencing (RNAseq) is a popular technique for unravelling the intricacies of gene activity. RNAseq experiments have many different research applications, and it can be challenging to appreciate all the design considerations, processing requirements, and limitations of RNAseq experiments as they apply to you. Join SIH bioinformaticians to equip yourself with this essential knowledge in our upcoming RNAseq training series. We’ll be covering experimental design, data processing methods, and all the essential computational considerations. 
On September 6th, Dr Nandan Deshpande will be presenting a webinar with the Australian BioCommons. He will guide us through the end-to-end process of RNAseq experiments, starting at the lab bench with RNA extraction through to the application of computational methods for gaining biological insights. Attendance is free and open to all. See the Australian BioCommons events page for registration details. 
If you’re looking for more hands-on experience doing RNAseq analysis on the command-line, join Dr Nandan Deshpande and Dr Georgie Samaha for a workshop on October 11-12th. This workshop is a part of the Australian BioCommons national training programme and participation is subject to application with selection. Applications close at 11:59pm AEST, Monday 25 September 2023. See the Australian BioCommons events page for registration details.
