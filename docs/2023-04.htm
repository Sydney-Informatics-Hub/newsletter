Highlights from the statistical consulting team:

With 2023 well under way, we are now back in full swing helping researchers and staff with their statistical queries. It has been really exciting to see a number of clients approach us at the experimental design stage, as they prepare for various grant proposals. 
We recently met with Dr Adeola Bamgboje-Ayodele to assist with a sample size calculation for a cluster randomised trial. Check out what she has said about the experience below: 
My experience with the Sydney Informatics Hub statistical consulting services was absolutely fantastic. It was my first time using their service and I was glad I did. I had a few meetings with two of their consultants (Alex Shaw and Ali Green) and they were both patient, helpful and insightful. They provided relevant resources, asked all the right questions, and even reviewed my clinical trial proposal. There was no stupid question to them. If you have any stats questions, they are definitely the best people to ask.

SIH Masterclass: A Hands-On Introduction to Git in RStudio 

27 April, 3-4:30pm: in-person or Zoom

This Masterclass is an opportunity for the attendees to bring their own laptop and R project and learn how to use Git and RStudio together for version control and collaboration. We will provide hands-on assistance in getting Git and RStudio to communicate effectively. 
The Masterclass will highlight the importance of making collaboration transparent for everyone, as well as the benefits of using version control to maintain a clear and comprehensive record of project development. We will provide the attendees with the tools and knowledge they need to collaborate more efficiently and keep their projects organized and reproducible.

Demystifying statistical modelling 

May 9 Hybrid workshops: in-person or Zoom 

Do you get confused about the differences between linear and logistic regression or ANOVA, ANCOVA, repeated measures and mixed models? On May 9 we will deliver three workshops from our modular statistical training system to demystify these terms and show how all of these align in the one Linear Models framework.
Linear Models Module 1 explains how the common analyses of regression, ANOVA, ANCOVA and repeated measures can all be done using the same Linear Models workflow. Module 2 then extends this to the Generalised Linear Model (GLM) framework for non-normal distributions such as Logistic and Count/Poisson regression. Aligning all these different methods into the same box with the same generic workflow makes it much easier to understand and remember how to apply them, while the practical examples provided give clear instructions for later use. The final module on the day, Statistical Model Building, explores the issues around more complex statistical modelling to build multivariable models with many predictors.
