SIH High Performance Computing Allocation Scheme 2024 - Round 2 applications now open


The Sydney Informatics Hub (SIH) HPC Allocation Scheme 2024 provides University of Sydney researchers access to time on the NCI Gadi supercomputer, one of the largest National HPC facilities in the Southern Hemisphere.
Compute resources available through this scheme will be allocated according to project merit and on a reasonable fair-share basis.
If you are looking for more compute or GPU power than Artemis can provide, this is a fantastic opportunity to get on to GADI.
Applications for Round 2 of the Scheme (for resources in Q3 and Q4, 2024) are open now, and close Tuesday 30th April.

[NEW] SIH Masterclass - Mastering Data Visualisation Design 

18 April, 3-4pm: online 


Data visualization is more than just making plots. It is a powerful way to showcase astounding results, communicate information, tell stories, and influence decisions. But how can we design impactful visuals that are clear, compelling, and captivating? 

In this Masterclass, we will showcase the principles of and best practices for data visualization design. We will explore how to choose the right visualisation type with a goal in mind and how to tailor images for a specific audience. We'll talk about how colour, shape, size, layout and interactivity can make our plots, infographics and charts stand out. 

Whether you're writing your Honours thesis, crafting your first paper or a seasoned researcher with an H-Index greater than 60, this workshop is for you (and just to be clear: this one has no coding involved)!

[NEW] Getting started with Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) in R

2 May, 9:30am-12pm: in-person 


Data can be messy and navigating its complexities can feel daunting. Every data frame is different, and data cleaning techniques can depend on personal data-wrangling preferences.
That’s where Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) comes in.  Rather than feeling overwhelmed by these unknowns or unsure of what really constitutes “clean” data, there are a few general steps you can take to ensure your data will be ready for your future analysis – no matter what you’re doing next.
In this brand-new workshop, we’ll demystify EDA and equip you with essential skills to confidently clean and explore your data. 

During this session, we will walk you through practical steps to prepare your data using real-world examples. If you have your own dataset, bring it along, and we will empower you to harness the power of EDA to drive your data-driven project forward. 

Don’t underestimate the impact of EDA.
Register now to secure your spot and unlock the true potential of your data!

Meta-Analysis for Systematic Reviews  


The importance of Systematic Review as a research method has grown considerably since the early 2000’s along with improvements in rigor provided by new quality standards, protocol registration and new methods and software for meta-analysis. 

We have been supporting the research community with training and consultancy support for meta-analysis for several years with interest really taking off in 2020.
Our training workshop “Meta-Analysis – An Introduction” consistently sold out during 2020.  We increased the sessions delivered from 3 to 5 in 2021, released more zoom tickets and still the sessions usually sold out. 
Registrations for this workshop went from 140 in 2020 to 289 in 2021, then 200 in 2022 and 298 in 2023. Now we are in 2024, and meta-analysis continues to be a popular topic for researchers across a broad variety of disciplines. 

Many researchers attend the introductory workshop then realise that there are still some tricky questions to solve and difficult judgements to make as they navigate the meta-analysis workflow. More and more researchers are now booking a consultation to discuss the more detailed aspects of meta-analysis. 
Let us assist you with strengthening your systematic review with a robust quality meta-analysis!
