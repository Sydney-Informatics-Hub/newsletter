Survival Analysis

5 July Hybrid workshop: in-person or Zoom 

Across the University of Sydney researchers in different disciplines are studying the time elapsed up to when a specified event occurs. For example, you might be interested in factors that influence survival of a person after a disease diagnosis or survival of a company after a stock market crash. Perhaps you like to study the survival time of marriage, or you are interested in how long a device or manufacturing component lasts before it fails or becomes obsolete. Survival analysis has also been applied to study staff churn or promotion, and the optimum time for soccer player substitution during a game.
To learn more about how ‘time-to event’/ survival analysis works, and how you can incorporate it into your research projects, sign up to the free Sydney Informatics Hub ‘Introduction to Survival Analysis’ workshop happening on the 5th of July.

Meta-Analysis

5 July Hybrid workshop: in-person or Zoom 

Are you currently working on a systematic review or plan to do so in the future? If yes, then it is important for you to know about meta-analysis, a statistical technique that can add a valuable quantitative component to your qualitative literature review. In contrast to primary studies that combine information across individual subjects, meta-analysis combines information across multiple studies.
This powerful technique can provide a more precise estimate of the quantitative effect of a particular phenomenon, whether it be an educational intervention, drug treatment, disease incidence in the human population, or anything else. In addition to increased accuracy, meta-analysis provides a quantitative snapshot of the existing research in your field, enabling you to compare the contribution of individual studies to an overall estimate. You can use the technique to better understand conflicting results across multiple studies by examining potential publication bias, and the effect of different population or study subgroups.
To learn more about how meta-analysis works, and how you can incorporate it into your research projects, sign up to the free Sydney Informatics Hub meta-analysis workshop on the 5th of July.
